§5.1 Angles

We frequently use the following Greek Letters for angle names in trigonometry.

Letter Name
\(\theta\) theta
\(\phi\) phi
\(\alpha\) alpha
\(\beta\) beta
\(\gamma\) gamma

We will be discussing angle between rays. A ray is

Standard Position is when we place the angle at the origin, and align the initial (AB of ABC) side with the \(x\)-axis pointing in the \(+x\) direction. The other ray (BC of ABC) is the is terminal side. Positive angles are counter-clockwise.

In addition to the normal degree system, we also use radians. The two most useful conversions are \(90º=\frac 12\pi\) in radians, and \(360º=2\pi\). A radian is the angle you get when you rap a string with the length of the radius of a circle around a cure and connect the outer point to the center to the other outer point on the at the other end of the string
